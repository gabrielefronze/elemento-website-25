What is Elemento?

Elemento is a Metacloud platform. Rather than replacing existing infrastructure, Elemento introduces a control and federation layer above private clouds, public clouds, sovereign clouds, hypervisors and Kubernetes environments.

The platform enables organisations to discover, govern, broker and orchestrate resources across heterogeneous providers through a unified operational model.

Elemento is the Metacloud: a sovereign control plane that lets organisations govern, orchestrate and optimise infrastructure across public clouds, private clouds, sovereign providers, hypervisors and native AtomOS environments without replacing what already exists.

Core Principle

Governance is decoupled from infrastructure ownership. Elemento federates what exists — it does not replace it.

Infrastructure independence

Retain ownership of workloads and data while governing across providers.

Vendor neutrality

No dependency on a single cloud, hypervisor or sovereign provider.

Digital sovereignty

Sovereignty becomes a workload policy, not a provider decision.

Workload portability

Move and broker workloads without application re-architecture.

Federation over standardisation

Join existing environments without forcing replatforming.

Incremental adoption

Connect first, govern next, optimise when ready.

Metacloud Architecture

Four functional zones: user access, control plane, interoperability and infrastructure.

1. User access

Admins, developers and operations teams interact through Electros.

2. Control plane

Electros federates resources through one governance and orchestration model.

3. Interoperability

C4 translates federation operations into provider-specific actions.

4. Infrastructure

Atomosphere and Atomosquare connect clouds, hypervisors and AtomOS.
